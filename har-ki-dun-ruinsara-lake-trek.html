Brief Description

The Har Ki Dun and Ruinsara Lake trip is a moderate, beginner-friendly hike. This journey will take you to the beautiful Har Ki Dun valley and the picturesque Ruinsara lake, which is situated among snow-capped mountains.

The trip takes you through lush woods of fir, pine, and rhododendron in the Govind National Park to reach the two sites, and it is a combination of pleasant stroll and challenging ascents. A section of the trail follows a river and passes through beautiful meadows. The journey begins in Har Ki Dun, also known as the Valley of Gods, which is located deep within the Govind Pashu National Park.

The route is covered in colorful orchids and hundreds of wildflowers, and it is recognized for its diverse flora and wildlife. The Ruinsara Lake, a holy lake on the opposite side of Swargarohini Peak from Har Ki Dun, is noted for its crystal clear water and snow-capped mountains as a background. The Black Peak, the highest peak in the Banderpunch Group of mountains, may also be seen on this hike.
