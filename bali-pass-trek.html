Brief Description

At a height of 16240 feet, the Bali Pass walk between Yamunotri and Har Ki Dun Valley is a challenging trip that experienced hikers should attempt.

The Bali Pass hike begins in Uttarakhand's Sankri hamlet and concludes at Janki Chatti. The Bandarpoonch, Kalanag, and Swargarohini peaks may all be seen from this trail. The Dev Thach alpine meadows, located in the heart of the Ruinsara forest, are worth seeing. The Ruinsara Lake is unsurpassed in its beauty and majesty, and it has a legendary narrative attached to it. Trekkers who want to experience nature at its finest and challenge their boundaries might consider the Bali Pass climb. May to June and September to October are the finest months to go on a Bali Pass hike.

To complete this journey successfully, one must have a high level of physical and mental stamina due to the difficult terrain, steep ascents and descents, thrilling mountain crossings, and limited paths.

Itinerary

Short Itinerary

  • Day 1: From Dehradun to Sankri (6-7 hrs)
  • Day 2: Drive from Sankri to Taluka and then trek to Seema Camp (7-8 hrs)
  • Day 3: From Seema Camp to Rainbasera trek (4-5 hrs)
  • Day 4: From Rainbasera to Ruinsara Tal Trek (4-5 hrs)
  • Day 5: From Ruinsara Tal to Odari (4-5 hrs)
  • Day 6: From Odari to Bali Pass Base Camp (3-4 hrs)
  • Day 7: From Bali Pass Base Camp to Lower Dhamni (9-10 hrs)
  • Day 8: From Lower Dhamni to Jaan ki Chatti via Yamunotri

You will be picked up from the Dehradun Railway Station at 06:30 a.m. today and taken to Sankri. It's a long journey over a beautiful route, with the final part passing through Govind Pashu National Park. At the evening, you'll arrive in Sankri. At Sankri, spend the night in tents or a guesthouse. The meal plan for today only includes supper, so bring extra cash for lunch on the way.

Report to the hamlet of Sankri, which is located in the Govind Pashu National Park. Sankri is a 6-7-hour journey from Dehradun, covering a distance of 197.2 kilometres. During your journey, you'll pass through a National Park with diverse flora and animals. Upon arrival, you will be assigned accommodations at a local guesthouse. Explore the local culture and customs to recharge your batteries after a long travel.

Drive from Sankri to Taluka in the early morning, which takes about an hour. At Taluka, you'll enjoy a hearty breakfast before starting your trek to Seema Camp. The trail takes you through lush forests of chestnuts, willows, and conifer trees, with scenic waterfalls along the way. You'll pass through Gangaar village before reaching Seema Camp, where you'll spend the night in tents under the stars. Depending on the weather, enjoyable activities will be planned.

After breakfast, you'll continue your trek along the Supin River. You'll reach Debshu Bugyal, which offers spectacular views of Kalanag peak. The trail descends to the Supin River Bridge before climbing again, leading to Rainbasera. Upon arrival, you'll camp overnight, surrounded by the beauty of the Milky Way in the night sky.

The trail ascends into the Ruinsara valley, with magnificent views of the surrounding peaks. You'll reach the serene Ruinsara Tal, a glacial lake encircled by meadows and rhododendrons. Spend the night in a wooden cabin, enjoying breathtaking views of Mt. Bandarpoonch, Kalanag, and White Peak.

Today's short trek takes you to Odari, which means "cave" in the local dialect and is believed to be Bali's shelter in the Ramayana. The trail transitions from lush greenery to rocky terrain. At Odari, you'll participate in a training session to prepare for the upcoming ascent. Overnight stay in tents with stunning mountain views.

The trip continues in the direction of the Bali Pass Base Camp. Along the journey, you must ascend a succession of ridges, which is extremely intimidating and difficult. The ridges are as sharp and steep as a knife's edge. Glaciers and stony debris blanket one side of the ridge. The first ridge may appear to be genuine. You must remember the trek guidelines and strictly adhere to our trek instructions. The steep ridges are angled at 60-70 degrees. You should take modest steps and stay in sync with your trip leader. You may also use your ice axe to keep the snow at bay. The excursion will allow you to experience the grandeur and emptiness of the pass, which is only encircled by a sea of clouds. Because the hike is short, it's best to stay active and hydrated to avoid getting AMS. During this journey, we reach a height of 15000 feet, and the temperature drops dramatically. It may also snow, which might make the journey more difficult. The chilling winds will undoubtedly give you the shivers. To avoid any type of health problem, you should layer yourself with appropriate clothes. We leave early after dinner for our search the next day.

The gorgeous dawn will begin early in the morning, and it will leave you speechless for a minute. We keep going until we reach the Bali pass, where we will camp for the night. The day starts at a fairly early hour. Following breakfast, the walk continues across the snowfields. To get farther, we'll employ ice and rock methods. Our trek guides will undoubtedly look after you. Because the portion is steep, the ascent is difficult. The really cold weather and the snow paths are both hard and fascinating. We get to enjoy the breathtaking vistas of the nearby hills. The route narrows to less than 6 inches in several spots. We arrive in Upper Dhamni and relax for a while before continuing on. We begin walking towards Lower Dhamni after a time. Lower Dhamni is a unique wooded region with steep hills. Tents will be used for the night.

The trek's final day has arrived. You can get up early in the morning for one final breath of fresh air, shoot a few photos as memories to look back on, and watch the last sunrise from the campground. As this is the last day of the journey, you will be served breakfast and certain activities will be conducted in groups. The route then continues for an hour through a beautiful forest, eventually leading to a paved trail that ascends all the way to Yamunotri. You may go to the popular Yamunotri shrine here. To wash away the tiredness, enjoy a relaxing swim in the boiling hot water springs of Yamunotri. We continue down the Yamuna, following the pilgrims' path to Jaan Ki Chatti, a tiny bustling village where we will spend the night in a guest home. The next day, you'll leave for Dehradun, bringing this incredible journey to a close.
