Brief Description

Kuari Pass Trek, also known as "The Lord Curzon Trail," is a spectacular trek located in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand. This trek offers a perfect blend of adventure and natural beauty, with stunning views of some of the highest peaks in India, including Nanda Devi, Chaukhamba, and Neelkanth. The trek passes through dense forests of oak and rhododendron, vast meadows, and charming villages.

At an altitude of 12,500 feet, Kuari Pass is famous for its panoramic views of the Himalayan ranges. The trek is ideal for both beginners and experienced trekkers, offering a moderate level of difficulty. During winter, the snow-covered trails add a magical charm to the journey, making it one of the most sought-after treks in Uttarakhand.

The trek starts from Rishikesh and takes you through scenic landscapes, including confluences of rivers and alpine forests. The highlight is reaching the Kuari Pass summit, where trekkers are rewarded with breathtaking views of the surrounding snow-clad peaks. This trek is a perfect escape for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts!
